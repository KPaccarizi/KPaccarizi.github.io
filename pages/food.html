Where to Eat

Kosovo offers a diverse dining scene with popular dining spots in cities like Pristina, Peja, Prizren, and Gjakova. From traditional Kosovo cuisine to international flavors, you can explore restaurants, cafes, and eateries to satisfy your taste buds. Rural areas and the scenic Rugova Valley also offer unique dining experiences. Remember, the best way to explore and discover restaurants in Kosovo is to ask locals for recommendations based on your preferences. They can provide valuable insights and guide you to hidden gems that may not be widely known.

Main Dishes

Kosovo has a rich culinary tradition influenced by various cultures and regional flavors. Include: sarma, pasul, qebapa, dollma, tave kosi etc. These dishes showcase the diverse and flavorful culinary traditions of Kosovo.

Desserts

Kosovo offers a delectable array of desserts to satisfy your sweet tooth. Here are some popular desserts in Kosovo: bakllava, shqerepare, hallve, tamloriz, kadaif, trilece, ravanije etc. These mouthwatering desserts reflect the sweet traditions of Kosovo and offer a delightful ending to any meal or a special treat on their own.

Drinks

Drinking coffee is part of a big tradition in Kosovo especially turkish coffee, other traditional drinks include black tea, boza, rasoj, ayran etc. Whether you're looking for traditional beverages or more familiar options, Kosovo has a diverse selection of drinks to cater to different tastes and preferences.

Pies and bread

Varieties of pies and bread are available throughout the country. Notably: flija, burek, pitalka, petulla, kifle, crepes. The cuisine of Kosovo reflects a combination of Balkan, Mediterranean, and Turkish influences, resulting in a diverse and flavorful culinary experience.
