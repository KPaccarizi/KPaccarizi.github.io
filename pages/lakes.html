Lakes in Kosovo

Lake Badovc

Situated near the town of Ferizaj, Lake Badovc is a tranquil reservoir surrounded by greenery. It provides a peaceful atmosphere and serves as a popular spot for walking, cycling, and bird-watching.

Lake Batlava

Located in the Sharri Mountains, Batlava Lake is a small glacial lake nestled in a picturesque valley. Surrounded by scenic landscapes, it offers a serene setting for hiking and enjoying the natural beauty of the region

Lake Gazivoda (Ujmani Lake)

Located in northern Kosovo, Lake Gazivoda is the largest lake in the country. It is shared with Serbia and offers a serene setting surrounded by forested hills. The lake is a popular destination for fishing, boating, and picnicking.

Radoniq Lake

Radoniq Lake is a beautiful artificial lake situated near the town of Lipjan. It provides a scenic backdrop for relaxation and leisure activities. Visitors can enjoy boating, fishing, and picnicking along the lakeside.

Drelaj Lake

Drelaj Lake is a small and serene lake situated in the Rugova Mountains in western Kosovo. Surrounded by dense forests and rocky cliffs, it offers a tranquil escape for nature lovers and hikers exploring the Rugova region.

Jazhinca Lake

Jazhincë Lake is a mountain lake in Kosovo, found in the eastern part of the Sharr Mountains. The elevation of this lake is 2’180 m/alt. Hiking to Jazhinca lake, it’s an easy and very beautiful daily hiking trip. Most of the hike is going through forests. This is a glacial lake, so it’s a very cold one. However, you can try to swim there! The heart-shape is visible from a rock around 50 m above the lake, where the rock covers a part of the lake. This trail is beautiful during all the seasons, especially in early summer. There are a lot of water sources all the way up, so take around 1 liter of water.

Vasileva Lake (Liqeni i Vasilevës)

Is a beautiful natural attraction located in the municipality of Viti (Vitina) in eastern Kosovo. It is a man-made lake formed by the construction of the Vasileva Dam on the Vardar River. Vasileva Lake spans across an area of approximately 13 square kilometers and is surrounded by picturesque landscapes, including rolling hills and dense forests. The tranquil waters of the lake offer a serene ambiance, making it a popular spot for relaxation, picnicking, and enjoying nature. The lake is also an ideal location for fishing enthusiasts, as it is home to various fish species, including carp, pike, and catfish. Fishing permits are available for those interested in angling in the lake's waters.
